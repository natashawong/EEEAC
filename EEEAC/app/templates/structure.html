STRUCTURE

The EEAC consists of an Executive Committee (EC), and Review Committees (RC) of various sizes on an as-needed basis.

Report Dissemination
Upon receiving a completed RC review, the EEAC will publicly disseminate the review and submit a copy to the EPA Administrator and other relevant EPA staff. RCs are highly encouraged to pursue publication in peer-reviewed outlets such as Science Policy Forum, Review of Environmental Economics and Policy. Prior to dissemination, final reviews should include the following disclaimer “This report was initiated by the Environmental Economics Advisory Committee (EEAC), an organization whose mission is to provide independent advice on the state-of-the-science with regard to the benefits, costs, and design of EPA’s environmental programs. The views expressed in this paper reflect those of the authors.”

Funding
Initial in-kind funding to develop a website will be provided by EC members. The EC may pursue additional funds from non-partisan sources to assist with the development and dissemination of RC reviews.

Guiding Principles
1. The EEAC will strive to provide EPA with the best available economic science as reflected in the current peer-reviewed literature.
2. The EEAC is a non-partisan, apolitical, body.
3. The EEAC's process, membership, and findings will be transparent and accessible to the public.
