Who we are

The External Environmental Economics Advisory Committee (E-EEAC) acts as an independent committee dedicated to providing up to date, non-partisan, economic research on environmental issues that pertain to EPA policy. The E-EEAC was established in the wake of the dissolution of the original EEAC, a scientific advisory committee under the Environmental Protection Agency, in June 2018. The members of the E-EEAC believe that, despite the retirement of the internal committee, the results of economic research remain crucial to the process of drafting economically feasible environmental policy.


Like its predecessor, the E-EEAC consists of economists and scientists who use their expertise to analyze the benefits and costs of environmental policy. The goal of the E-EEAC is to operate in this capacity until the EPA is ready to reconstitute an internal economic advisory committee. While the structure of the E-EEAC has changed, the members of this committee are dedicated to ensuring that relevant environmental economic research is readily available to the public on this website
